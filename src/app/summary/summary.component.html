Executive Summary

To ensure Blockchain can be a secure technology of the future, it is important to investigate and understand the security limitations and vulnerabilities of Blockchain. The scope of this research includes the security of Blockchain, why it is secure or insecure, and possible improvements to the cybersecurity of Blockchain. The research will not address alternate technology to Blockchain, detailed methods to utilise Blockchain and the specifics of Blockchain programming. This research examines the currency, relevance, authority, accuracy and purpose to address the reliability of the sources.

It was found that Blockchain vulnerabilities can result in colossal losses of money, or even cause the collapse of a business. Most system implementations of Blockchain technology are focused exclusively on the cryptocurrency industry resulting in colossal windfalls to hackers upon a successful cyberattack. Blockchain only guarantees the security of the system data inside the Blockchain from external threats. Therefore, failing to adequately protect the system’s boundaries will increase the system’s attack surface, increasing the probability of an attack. The annual growth rate of hacking incidents and their loss targeted at Blockchain systems exceeded all other types of IT systems during their technology maturity periods. Furthermore, the research found that the rate of cybersecurity incidents related to Blockchain has increased.

It is postulated that the current users of Blockchain generally feel that the system they use is safer and more secure than previously hacked systems. However, numerous organisations think lack of security is a barrier to implementing Blockchain within their business. It is questioned whether Blockchain is improving at an acceptable rate to be used for large amounts of cryptocurrency as it appears that the cybersecurity of Blockchain is improving at a much lower rate than other IT systems. This analysis concludes that there are several security gaps and vulnerabilities that need to be considered when designing Blockchain systems. Blockchain also requires further research to increase protection against cyberattacks. Recommendations include organisations that implement Blockchain following a cybersecurity framework, investing in a team of cybersecurity professionals and following best practices and standards. Recommendations also include increasing research into the cybersecurity of Blockchain and to increase research to help the investors of Blockchain make informed decisions.
